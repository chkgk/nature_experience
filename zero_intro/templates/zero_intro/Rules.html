You are randomly matched with a co-player. Your co-player is not a human, but a computer. Your play does not affect the computer’s decision.

Three factors together determine your payoff from this decision:
(1) The draw of a ball from an urn,
(2) your own decision, and
(3) the decision by your co-player.

The urn from which the ball is drawn contains 100 balls. {{ red_balls }} balls are red, {{ green_balls }} are green.
If a red ball is drawn, you receive nothing (US$ 0) irrespective of your and your co-player’s choice. So does your co-player.
If a green ball is drawn, then your outcome depends on your and your co-player’s decision (see below). You will not know the outcome of the draw from the urn at the time of taking your decision.

For your own decision, you have a choice between two options: Either you choose option A or option B. Your co-player has the same choice.
These are the possibilities:

• A green ball is drawn and you choose option A and your co-player chooses option A: You receive US$ 1. So does your co-player.
• A green ball is drawn and you choose option A and your co-player chooses option B: You receive US$ 1. Your co-player receives US$ 3.
• A green ball is drawn and you choose option B and your co-player chooses option A: You receive US$ 3. Your co-player receives US$ 1.
• A green ball is drawn and you choose option B and your co-player chooses option B: You receive nothing (US $ 0). So does your co-player.

To repeat: If a red ball has been drawn, you receive nothing (US$ 0). [if human]
Based on previous sessions of this experiment, a co-player chooses A in about 60 out of 100 cases and B in about 40 out of 100 cases.

[else, if computer]
On average, a computer chooses A in about 60 out of 100 cases and B in about 40 out of 100 cases.
